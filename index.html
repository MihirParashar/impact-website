Abstract

In recent years, carbon emissions have become increasingly dangerous to Earthâ€™s ecosystem and environment. Consequently, countries around the world have been putting in measures to counteract excessive carbon emissions and help reduce carbon emissions. However, access to these technologies is expensive, and low-income areas may not be able to reduce their carbon footprint. Understanding the correlation between household income and carbon footprint is crucial to reducing inequalities and creating sustainable communities, fulfilling the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 11. This research project aims to determine the correlation, if any, between the average household income and the average carbon footprint of a geographical area. It is hypothesized that lower-income areas will have higher average carbon footprints due to factors such as inefficient housing, reliance on fossil fuels, and limited access to sustainable infrastructure. The research will utilize publicly available datasets on household income and carbon emissions by county and ZIP code. Using the results, a solution can be proposed to decrease the overall carbon footprint by specifically targeting the areas with a higher carbon footprint through cheap, preventative software.
